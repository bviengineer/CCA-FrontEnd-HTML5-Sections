Coding Apps on Mobile Devices and Tablets

MIT App Inventor

As the name implies, MIT App Inventor was created by Massachusetts Institute of Technology and is an open source initiave that enables young children through adults to create fully funcitonal apps. Being a visual-based program, App Inventor concludes that apps can be built in as little as a half hour. Most noteably, MIT App Invetor is at no cost.

Kodeable

Kodeable teaches computer science concepts via games and can be used on the web or on an iPad. The app is designed for children ages 4 - 10. There is a free plan and two fee-based plans.

Pyonkee

Pyonkee is a visual-based programming app for iPads and is derived from the MIT Media Lab Scratch program. Scrath based programs are compatible with Pyonkee.

Lightbot

Lightbot is a puzzle-based app that encourages kids to use logic, a skill needed in computer science, to solve the puzzles. There are junior coding puzzles for children ages 4 - 8 and puzzles geared towards children 9 and older. The Junior puzzles are available in the Android, Kindle and Apple stores and the app for the older elmentary and onward age range, is availale in the stores mentioned previously in addition to the Windows and Mac stores.

What Is S.T.E.M.

S.T.E.M is the acronym for Science Technology Engineering Math. The U.S. Department of Education provides a great outline of why S.T.E.M. learning is important.
