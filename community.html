Organizations That Specialize in Extra Cirriular Programming

100 Girls of Code

100 Girls of Code is a chapter-based non-profit organization with the goal of achieving gender equality in S.T.E.M. by offering accessible workshops in computer science and engieering to young women. To attend a workshop, girls can visit their local chapter's website for upcoming opportunities, sign-up and attend.

Girls Who Code

Girls Who Code is a naitonal non-profit committed to narrowing the gender ineuality in technolgy through after school clubs for 6th thru 12th grade girls and and a 7-week summer immersion program. Clubs are free and meet at various locations such as the library, schools and community centers.

Georgia Science and Robotics Academy

Georgia Science and Robotics Academy is a non-profit with a mission to teach children in elmentary through high school to be proficient in each area of S.T.E.M. Fun Weired Science is the known name for Academy and engages students through interactive and hands-on acitivies and demonstrations, parties, S.T.E.M. summer camps, after school programs. Workshops, lesson plan preparation and cohort-style learning are also availbale for Teachers on a short-term or ongoing basis.

CoderDojo

CoderDoJo belives that everyone should be able to the opportunity to learn fundamentals of computer science from an early age given the importance of technology in todays arena. Their club-based organization is led by volunteers and is open to the community at no cost.

What Is S.T.E.M.

S.T.E.M is the acronym for Science Technology Engineering Math.
